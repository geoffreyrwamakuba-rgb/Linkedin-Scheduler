Your dashboard says the API averages 120ms.

Your biggest customer just waited 4 seconds.

Both are true.

That's the problem with averages: they describe a system nobody actually experiences.

Percentiles describe what real requests feel:

p50 — the median. Half of requests are faster, half slower. Your "typical" experience.

p95 — 1 request in 20 is slower than this.

p99 — 1 in 100. Sounds rare.

It isn't. Here's the maths nobody runs:

If one page load fires 100 API calls, the chance of hitting your p99 at least once is about 63%.

Your "1% worst case" is most sessions' normal.

And the slow tail isn't random, either.

The heaviest requests usually come from your biggest customers — the accounts with the most data are the ones living at p99.

So the average reports the experience of nobody.

p99 reports the experience of the customers you least want to lose.

Never cross a river because it's "on average" a metre deep.
